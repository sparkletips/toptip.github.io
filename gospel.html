CAPTIVITY AND RETURN: DECREE THAT ENGENDER FREEDOM FROM CAPTIVITY.
When the seventy years prophesied for the Babylonian captivity was up (see Jer.25:11-12; 29:10; Dan.9:2); God raised Emperor Cyrus who issued a decree authorizing any willing Jew to return to Jerusalem and join in rebuilding the temple (see 2Chron.36:22-23; Ezra 1:1-4). That written decree carried the authority that freed the Jews from their many years captivity and then empowered them to return and rebuild. What is a decree? The Oxford Advanced Learner's English Dictionary defines decree as 'an official order from a ruler or a government that becomes the law.' It is the expression of the king's authority in words. For emperors then, who were absolute authority and power, their decrees were sacrosanct. They were neither negotiable nor debatable. As soon as words expressing such decrees leap out of their mouth, they are obeyed with speed. Peradventure such decrees gets documented, it seals the matter making it immutable.


The Scripture recognizes this. It says "Where the word of a king is, there is power: and who may say unto him, what doest thou?" (Eccl.8:4 cp. Est.7:8-10). Kings (particularly then) are different from government officials today. Most governments today are democratic and so does not rest on one man's shoulder. The powers of the state are diffused. The man who makes the law (Legislature) is not the one who interprets it (Judiciary). Neither of the two implements same law (Executive). These are checks and balances introduced to engender fairness. Any of them who errs in carrying out their duties can be called to order and if need be punished or removed.


Where the word of a king is, there is power: and who may say unto him, what doest thou?

Eccl.8:4
The then emperors and kings (and in some places today) are like God. "For the LORD is our Judge, the LORD is our Lawgiver, the LORD is our King…" (Isa.33:32). Only God is just and fair enough to combine the three arms of government. The kings then arrogated that power to themselves. When they speak, their words can set a captive free. This was the instrument God used to free the Jews from the Babylonian captivity. If the words of a man can be that powerful, can you imagine the power in the word of God?


This is the beginning of a new year- a strategic one. As we head towards the end of time, men because of the pressures of the shakings of the end-time will come under increasing captivity. Apostle Paul captured it this way. "Now the Spirit speaketh expressly, that in the latter times some shall depart from the faith, giving heed to seducing spirits, and doctrines of devils, speaking lies in hypocrisy; having their conscience seared with a hot iron" (1Tim.4:1-2). "This know also, that in the last days perilous times shall come" (2 Tim.3:1). Pressures and pain are going to characterize life on earth. Owing to this, men will come under many vices and ungodly lifestyles to either find solution to problems or ease the sufferings inflicted by them.


Some will find escape in drinks and end up getting addicted to alcohol. Some others will resort to smoking to brave their moments. There will be those who will do drugs to find apparent relief. The devil will ensure men never run out of opium. One thing is sure, it does not take long after one starts abusing these substances before he gets addicted to them. To anything one is addicted to, he becomes a captive of (Jn.8:34; Rom.6:16).


Gambling, fornication, adultery, lesbianism, homosexuality and other sexual perversions are on the increase today. One primary reason for this is economic pressures and pains and the quest for survival. When one's back is against the wall, he/she can yield to anything just to survive. There is literarily no difference between the attire of harlots and that of most women in modern society. Hidden behind what is called 'trendy dressing' is a polluted mindset of sexual seduction masterminded from the pit of hell to trap men in the hex of sex and promote pornography and prostitution.
